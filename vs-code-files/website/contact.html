On 13 November 1985, Nevado Del Ruiz erupted killing about 25,000 in a town of 30,000 inhabitants. Marta Lucía Calvache Velasco, is the technical director of the Colombian Geological Service, she and her colleagues had submitted a report describing the history of the site 1 month before the eruption. The reports were ignored multiple times.

"Gabrielina Ferruccio says in a film that when ash started falling on Armero on the eve of the eruption, she went to church to ask for advice, the priest told her to “enjoy this beautiful show, it will never be seen again.”

Edilma Loiza says she remembers, "at 6 p.m. a fire truck went through town telling everybody to stay at home, to not leave home or panic.” The lahars or avalanches of volcanically induced landslides and debris flows, only occurred 5 hours later.

Colombian volcanology grew exponentially thanks to international aid that arrived after the disaster and helped establish a network of observatories. There are currently 600 observatories monitoring Nevado Del Ruiz. There have also been over 14 hazard maps created.

In June 1989, Nevado del Ruiz had an eruption similar to the one in 1985, that destroyed Armero. As soon as one of the new monitoring stations detected an increase in seismic activity, the SGC produced daily updates on the status of the volcano. On 30 August, the SGC told the local community that an eruption was imminent and evacuations were necessary.
