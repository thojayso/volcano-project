In recorded history, Ruiz had two more notable eruptive events. In 1595, three Plinian eruptions killed 636 people, when a lahar swept down the nearby river valleys. 250 years later, mudflows again flooded the upper valley of the Lagunillas River, killing more than 1,000 people. November of 1984, climbers on the mountain reported gas emerging from the summit crater. In December, three earthquakes were felt within 20 miles of Nevado Del Ruiz. This activity continued into the next year.
